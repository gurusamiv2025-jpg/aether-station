# JOSC Charter — Excerpts Relevant to Aether Station

## Article 4: Crew & Command

The commander of an orbital research station holds final authority over
operational decisions affecting crew safety, life support, and reactor
status. The Joint Orbital Sciences Charter cedes to the commander the
right to override automated isolation routines (Mira-7 and its
successors are forbidden by Article 4.2 from reactor controls without
explicit human authorization), and the duty to log any such override
within thirty minutes.

Cmdr. Park has invoked Article 4 once in her current tenure: during
the LiF-B coolant leak of 2096-02-14, when she ordered a manual
isolation that ran ahead of Mira-7's automated sequence by eleven
seconds.

## Article 7: Research Conduct

JOSC research conducted aboard a station is the joint property of the
sponsoring institutions and the on-station team. Disputes between
on-station scientists and Earth-side principal investigators are
adjudicated by the station commander acting as on-scene reviewer. No
sample (organic or otherwise) may be transported off-station without
both the commander's sign-off and Mira-7's containment certification.

This article is the one Dr. Okafor cites when JOSC asks him to ship
sample HB-441 back to Cape Canaveral. He cites it slowly.

## Article 12: Comms

A 4-hour comms blackout requires a written incident report; a 24-hour
blackout triggers an automatic JOSC review. The 2095-11-22 blackout
fell at four hours seventeen minutes. Park's report ran to nine pages
and was, in her own words, "the most boring thing I have ever written."
