# Aether Station — General

Aether Station is a third-generation research platform in a 14-hour polar
orbit around Theia, a sub-Neptune moon of the gas giant Halberd-IV. The
station was commissioned in 2089 by the Joint Orbital Sciences Charter (JOSC)
and is currently on its seventh six-month research rotation.

## Physical specifications

- Habitable volume: 11,400 m³ across four pressurized rings
- Crew complement: 12 nominal, 5 during the current "minimum science" cycle
- Power: dual fission core (Reactor A primary, Reactor B reserve), 14 MW peak
- Coolant: pressurized lithium-fluoride loop (LiF-A through LiF-D)
- Comms: 11-minute average light-lag to Earth ground stations

## Operational status

The station is currently operating on **Reactor A only**, following the
February 2096 LiF-B coolant leak (see `lore/incidents/coolant-leak.md`).
Reactor B will remain offline until the next supply run, ETA Q4 2096.

## Notable facilities

- Ring 1: Command, comms, observation deck
- Ring 2: Crew quarters, mess, medbay
- Ring 3: Xenobiology labs, sample isolation suite
- Ring 4: Reactor hall, machine shop, EVA airlock
