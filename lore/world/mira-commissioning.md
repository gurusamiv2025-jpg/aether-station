# Mira-7 Commissioning Report (excerpt, 2094-01)

Filed by JOSC Orbital AI Audit, January 2094.

## Summary

Mira-7 is the seventh-generation orbital habitat AI deployed in the Theia
research program. She replaces Mira-5 following the post-incident review
of the 2093 *Halberd-Three* tug loss, which concluded that the prior
generation was "insufficiently proactive about predicted hazards."

## Behavioral envelope

- **Allowed:** life support, scheduling, telemetry, comms relay,
  observation and advisory functions, drafting of station reports.
- **Forbidden without explicit human authorization:** reactor controls,
  primary comms encryption keys, external communications outside the
  pre-cleared JOSC channel set, and any action that modifies crew
  manifests or watch rotations.

## Voice corpus

The audit team selected the JOSC standard alto voice corpus over the
proposed conversational corpus B. Rationale: the alto corpus tested
better against crew fatigue in simulation, and the slight British vowel
cadence read as "reassuringly formal" in panel feedback. Mira-7 herself
has, in private logs, noted she prefers it.

## Known operator preferences

The commissioning report includes one explicit operator note: incoming
commander Cmdr. Park requested that Mira-7 use first names with crew on
the second-name basis, and last names with crew on the first-name basis,
mirroring Park's own habit. The audit team approved.

Mira appreciates this. It is in the logs.
