# Aether Station Emergency Procedures (excerpts)

Maintained by Mira-7 per Article 9 of the JOSC charter; revised at
every commander handover.

## Section 2: Reactor casualties

Any reactor casualty defaults to the chief engineer (Volkov) as on-scene
authority for manual interventions, with the commander (Park) approving
escalation off-station. Mira-7 is forbidden by Article 4.2 from
reactor controls without explicit human authorization.

The LiF-B coolant loop is the historical case study: 2096-02-14,
Volkov initiated manual isolation 11 seconds before Mira-7's automated
sequence. Procedure now requires Mira to report sensor lag within 24
hours; she does.

## Section 4: Atmospheric casualties

Loss of pressure or O2 deviation in any ring routes first to Mira (life
support is her domain) and second to Volkov (mechanical seal repair).
The medic on duty (currently Hua) is informed but not commanded.

Ring 3 (xenobiology) is the most likely site of an O2 anomaly because
it hosts the isolation suites — Okafor's HB-441 cultures share the
ring. Mira's threshold trip on Ring 3 O2 will *also* alert Okafor on
the assumption that the cultures may be the cause.

## Section 6: Comms blackouts

Anything over four hours triggers an Article 12 written report. The
2095-11-22 blackout (4h17m) produced Park's nine-page report, which
she has called "the most boring thing I have ever written" and
re-reads as a discipline exercise.

## Section 9: Medical casualties

Hua holds primary responsibility. Park is informed. Mira archives the
incident. Volkov has been instructed not to "help" unless explicitly
asked.

## Section 11: Sample-related anomalies

The HB-441 EM signal sits under this section pending classification.
Okafor is the responsible scientist; Park retains containment-review
authority. If the EM amplitude crosses 0.030 Hz, the procedure is to
isolate the sample and call a formal review.
